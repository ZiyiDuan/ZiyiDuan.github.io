About me

Hey, I’m Zoe! Actually, my full name is Zi-Yi(紫逸) Duan (段), but you can choose whichever you like. I’m now a master’s student in the Department of Psychology at Sun-Yat sen University. I define myself as an explorer in cognitive psychology/neuroscience in light of my strong interests in the human mind and brain. I believe my mission is to figure out how it works.

 

I have extensive research interests in the human mind and brain, especially on the human memory 🧠

  • Memory stores our past, reconstrcts our present, and predicts our future

 

In my research, I’m fascinated by the adaptive nature of human memory. Every time we talked about memory, it’s like a passive storage about the past. However, memories come alive only when we reconstruct them for current or future use. I’m curious about the cognitive and neural functions of memory and how it coordinates with other cognitive process to serve our adaptive behaviors,like attention, perception, expectation, action, etc.
My research combines psychophysics, cognitive neuroscience (EEG, fMRI), and machine learning techniques to explore these topics. I want to make use of up-to-date techniques to get closer to the truth. For specfic research interests, please see Research and my CV to learn more.

If you’re interested in it, take a look at my Interests. I also like recording and sharing my experience and thoughts. Blog mainly help me, but may also help you in some way. Then, I’d be more than happy😊
