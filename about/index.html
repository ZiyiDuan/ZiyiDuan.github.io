About me

Hey, I’m Zoe! Actually, my full name is Zi-Yi(紫逸) Duan (段), but you can choose whichever you like. I’m a second-year master’s student in the Department of Psychology at Sun-Yat sen University. I define myself as an explorer in cognitive science/neuroscience in light of my strong interests in the human mind and brain. Given the constrain of limited cognitive resources, many seemingly simple human behaviors are very smart and adaptive. Therefore, I’m eager to explore how the tiny, cute brain guides our behavior in such an intelligent way!

 

I have extensive research interests on human cognition, but mainly focus on the vision👀 and memory🧠

  • Eyes as windows into memory
  • Memory makes who we are

 

In my research, I’m a question-driven girl. I wonder how do we perceive and represent the real-world with limited cognitive resources? How do we encode, store, and organize our memory in an adaptive way? How do we use our memory to guide our behavior and interact with others? I’m open-minded about new techniques, and I always feel excited about learning new skills! During my past experience, I used behavioral experiments, eye-tracking, and Bayesian modeling to investigate human behaviors. More recently, I gained the experience of combining machine-learning and neuroimaging techniques in LewPeaLab. Please see my CV and Research to learn more.

 

Outside of research, I’m a boring gril…just kidding😄 I like taking photos, drawing pictures, watching videos, making healthy and delicious food, meeting new people, and trying new things! If you’re interested in it, take a look at my Interests. I also like recording and sharing my experience and thoughts. Blog mainly help me, but may also help you in some way. Then, I’d be more than happy😊
